# Content Model — Next.js System

This document defines expected page/content types for SEO-first App Router projects generated with this system.

---

## Content model goals

- Keep page types consistent across projects.
- Enable predictable SEO behavior per type.
- Reuse route/config/UI patterns instead of ad hoc page structures.

---

## Static pages

Examples: Home, About, Company, Careers overview.

- Usually route-level static content under `src/app/.../page.tsx`.
- Require clear intent, one logical `h1`, and metadata.
- Often include reusable sections (hero, value props, CTA, FAQ).

SEO notes: unique title/description, strong internal linking to key conversion/content pages.

---

## Article / blog detail pages

Examples: `/blog/[slug]`, `/insights/[slug]`.

- Use dynamic route segments with path helpers (e.g. `paths.article(slug)`).
- Render article title, publication context, and body content in semantic structure.
- Support related content linking and optional breadcrumbs.

SEO notes: canonical URL per slug, article metadata, OG/Twitter, `Article` JSON-LD where applicable.

---

## Article listing pages

Examples: `/blog`, `/insights`.

- Provide filter/sort UX only if needed and indexability-safe.
- List items using reusable article card patterns.
- Keep pagination/discoverability clear.

SEO notes: meaningful intro copy, stable metadata, internal links to details and key categories.

---

## Category pages

Examples: `/blog/category/[slug]`, service categories.

- Represent grouped content intent (topic, industry, use case).
- Include category heading/description plus linked item cards.
- Avoid thin tag pages with no editorial context.

SEO notes: unique category metadata, canonicalized routing, noindex for low-value autogenerated buckets when necessary.

---

## Service pages

Examples: `/services/[slug]`, `/consulting`.

- Compose reusable sections: hero, outcomes, proof, process, CTA, FAQ.
- Keep content conversion-oriented but structurally semantic.
- Link to relevant case studies/articles/contact.

SEO notes: distinct title/description by service intent, optional `FAQPage` or service-relevant schema when accurate.

---

## Legal pages

Examples: Privacy Policy, Terms, Cookies.

- Stable routes and footer visibility.
- Usually static, text-heavy, and low interaction.
- Keep update timestamps/version notes if required by policy.

SEO notes: typically indexable unless legal/privacy requirements state otherwise.

---

## Contact pages

Examples: `/contact`.

- Include clear contact intent, trust context, and form/channel options.
- Validate form data server-side; add spam/bot protections.
- Keep success/failure states user-safe and non-leaky.

SEO notes: local/business metadata where relevant, internal links from high-intent pages.

---

## Cross-type requirements

All page types should:

- Use `src/lib/paths.ts` for route references.
- Use `src/lib/metadata.ts` and `src/lib/schema.ts` helpers.
- Reuse shared layout/section patterns from `components/`.
- Respect single sources of truth in `src/config/*`.
